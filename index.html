How It Works

Four Steps From Vault to USDC

Patients stay in control at every step. Researchers pay for access. Everything is logged on Base with sub-cent EIP-4844 blob transactions.

1

Create Your Vault

Deploy an ERC-7579 smart account on Base via Coinbase Smart Wallet — no seed phrases. Upload FHIR-formatted lab results and wearable exports to IPFS, AES-256 encrypted with keys split via Shamir's Secret Sharing.

2

Researcher Posts Query

A biotech team specifies ICD-10 codes, age brackets, and biomarker thresholds on-chain. USDC is locked in escrow. Your anonymized vault metadata is checked for match — raw records never exposed.

3

You Approve Access

You receive a permissioned disclosure request and sign a time-scoped, revocable ERC-20 access token granting decrypted read rights. Consent is minted as an ERC-7231 identity-bound attestation on Base.

4

Earn & Audit

USDC escrow auto-releases to your wallet on confirmed token generation. Every grant and revocation is on-chain — your tamper-proof audit trail satisfies FDA RWE compliance requirements.

Architecture

Built for Trust at Every Layer

No single-party key control: AES-256 keys are split with Shamir's Secret Sharing — reconstruction requires patient cooperation, preventing unauthorized access even if IPFS storage is compromised.

~$0.001 per consent log: EIP-4844 blobs on Base make it viable to mint an ERC-7231 attestation for every individual consent event — impossible at L1 gas prices.

Revocable access tokens: Time-scoped ERC-20 tokens expire automatically. Patients can revoke at any time, with on-chain attestation of revocation for the audit trail.

Planned
